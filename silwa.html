THE COMMON METHODS OF INTERNET/ MEDIA SCAMS

Phishing:

This is a form of internet fraud in which an attacker masquerades as a reputable entity or person (e.g. MD, Accountant, etc.) in email or other channels of communication, and try to obtain financial and/or other confidential info such as passwords, BVN, and credit card numbers from internet users, typically by sending an email that looks as if it is from a legitimate organization, usually a bank, but such email contains a link to a fake website trap that replicates the real one quoted. These identity thieves send emails that lack domain, show silly mistakes, and manipulated version of the organization or person cloned. Like the fisherman, they throw the baits into the cyberspace for possible victims to catch.

Smishing:

This is derived ex. SMS and PHISHING an SMS type of phishing. It is a fraudulent practice of sending biased bulk text messages purporting to be from your bank or a regulator asking you for personal or financial information such as your account or card details. Clue: they usually don’t mention any bank name because they can’t guess your bank rightly.

Social Engineering:

This is the term used for a broad range of malicious activities accomplished directly through human interactions. It uses psychological manipulation to trick users into making security mistakes or giving away sensitive information. For instance, the Ponzi and pyramid schemes. These schemes are fraudulent investing scams promising high rates of return with little risk to investors.

Identity Theft:

The deliberate use of someone else’s identity by a scammer, such as name, signature, company logo, stamp, credit card, passport, ID card, etc to gain financial advantage or obtain credit, receive contract, gain passage or acceptance and other intended benefits in the other person’s name, and to the detriment and loss of the latter and thus avert apprehension.

Email Attachments:

Scammers may claim you have payment to collect and suggest you click/ open the attached file(s) to view the details of the invoice, payment slip or other docs. Just delete the mail and never click on the suggested link.
